BIO

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student in Political Science at Washington University in St. Louis. I study comparative politics and political behavior. Specifically, I am interested in authoritarian politics, government censorship, propaganda, popular protest, and political repression. Methodologically, I use a wide range of research methods, including formal modeling, observational analysis, and randomized experiments. I have regional expertise in the Greater China area and Southeast Asia.

My recent paper on censorship in China proposes a new way of understanding censorship and authoritarian control. My study highlights normalization as a powerful strategy of authoritarian government to control the society and reduce backlash caused by repressive policies.

In summer 2021, I will receive my A.M. degree in Political Science from Washington University in St. Louis. Prior to joining Washington University, I received an LL.B. degree in Law from Renmin University of China.
